# Gameplans

Gameplans are the strategy layer that sits next to your playbooks.

## Offensive Gameplans

Offensive gameplans control:

- Tempo
- Attack style
- Personnel usage
- Target distribution

That means your offense can lean on short throws, stretch the field, milk the clock, or spread the ball around even before the playbook resolves a specific snap.

## Defensive Gameplans

Defensive gameplans control:

- Pressure
- Coverage shell
- Run fit
- Situational focus

That lets you decide whether the defense should sit back, attack the pocket, tighten up the middle of the field, or sell out to create turnovers.

## What The Settings Mean

- A slower offensive tempo lowers volume and helps protect a lead.
- Up-tempo football raises pace and can wear down opponents.
- Ball-control styles improve short-game stability.
- Vertical styles trade stability for explosive upside.
- Aggressive defenses can create big plays, but they also open themselves up to busts.

## How This Differs From Playbooks

- Playbooks choose the play pool.
- Gameplans bias how those plays are used.
- Fit scoring checks whether the roster supports the result.

In practice, the strongest teams align all three:

1. Personnel
2. Playbook
3. Gameplan

If those three disagree, the roster usually gives you away sooner or later.
