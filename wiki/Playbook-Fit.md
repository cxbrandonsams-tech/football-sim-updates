# Playbook Fit

Playbook fit measures how well your roster supports the plays you are calling.

## The Score

- Fit is scored from `0.0` to `1.0`.
- The game uses the top healthy players at the relevant positions.
- Offensive fit is based on the active offensive playbook.
- Defensive fit is based on the roster shape of your defensive starters.

Neutral fit is around `0.65`.

## What It Affects

Playbook fit feeds two different parts of the game:

- Play selection weighting
- In-simulation success bonuses or penalties

That means a good fit makes the play more likely to be called and more likely to work once it is called.

## Rating Demand Examples

- Inside runs care most about offensive line run blocking plus RB power and vision.
- Outside runs care about line blocking plus RB speed and elusiveness.
- Short passing cares about QB short accuracy, WR route running, and OL pass blocking.
- Medium passing leans on QB medium accuracy plus WR route running and TE hands.
- Deep passing leans on QB deep accuracy, arm strength, and WR speed.

## Letter Grades

| Fit | Grade |
|---|---|
| 0.90+ | A+ |
| 0.85-0.89 | A |
| 0.80-0.84 | B+ |
| 0.75-0.79 | B |
| 0.70-0.74 | C+ |
| 0.65-0.69 | C |
| 0.55-0.64 | D |
| Below 0.55 | F |

## Why It Matters

The game is built so that scheme matters, but it does not override talent:

- A strong fit can create a real edge.
- A bad fit creates a real penalty.
- The best teams line up their roster, their playbook, and their gameplan instead of fighting themselves.
