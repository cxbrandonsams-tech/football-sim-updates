# Hall of Fame

Era-relative legacy scoring system that evaluates player careers for Hall of Fame induction and team-level Ring of Honor recognition.

## Hall of Fame

**Threshold:** 175 points for induction.

### 6 Scoring Components

1. **Seasonal ranks** — How the player ranked among peers each season.
2. **Awards** — MVP, All-Pro, and other accolades.
3. **Championships** — Rings and championship game appearances.
4. **Longevity** — Years of productive play.
5. **Career stats** — Cumulative statistical achievements.
6. **All-time rank** — Where the player stands on all-time leaderboards.

Scoring is era-relative, meaning a dominant player from an earlier era is evaluated against their contemporaries rather than absolute numbers.

## Ring of Honor

Team-specific recognition with two tiers:

| Tier | Threshold | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Ring of Honor induction | 85 points | Team-level recognition |
| Jersey retirement | 130 points | Highest team honor |

Ring of Honor scoring includes a **loyalty bonus** for players who spent significant portions of their career with one franchise.

## Legacy Tiers

Players are classified into tiers from lowest to highest:

- No Case
- Borderline
- Good Case
- Strong Case
- Hall of Famer
