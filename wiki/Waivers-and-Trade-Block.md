# Waivers And Trade Block

Waivers and the trade block are both ways to move players without jumping straight to a trade.

## Waivers

When a player is cut during the regular season, they spend one week on waivers before becoming a free agent.

Priority works like this:

- Before the trade deadline, the worst teams get first crack.
- After the trade deadline, priority becomes rolling.

You also need an open roster spot to claim a player.

## How Claims Resolve

- AI teams make claims based on roster need and whether the player is an upgrade.
- If multiple teams claim the same player, the highest-priority valid claimant wins.
- If nobody claims the player, they clear to free agency.

## Trade Block

The trade block is the player-shopping layer.

- Mark a player as available.
- Other teams see interest based on position need and roster weakness.
- The game shows the teams most likely to care.

The trade block does not generate autonomous AI trade proposals. It only surfaces interest.

## Why It Matters

These systems give you a middle layer between "keep the player" and "trade the player":

- Waivers create risk when you cut someone.
- The trade block lets you test the market before you commit.
