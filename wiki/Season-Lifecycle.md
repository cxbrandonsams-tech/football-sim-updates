# Season Lifecycle

Every season in Football Sim runs through the NFL calendar, but the offseason is split into smaller windows so roster and coaching decisions matter.

---

## Regular Season

The core loop is still weekly:

1. Set your gameplan
2. Manage your roster
3. Play or sim your game
4. Read the news
5. Advance the week

### During the season

- Scouting opens and scouting budgets are spent across the year.
- Waivers last one week.
- The trade block shows who is actually shopping a player.
- Trading stays available during the regular season.
- The college season advances one week at a time alongside your own league.

## Playoffs

- 7 teams per conference qualify.
- Wild Card, Divisional, Conference Championship, then the league title game.
- Higher seeds get home advantage.
- Interviews happen here, which is the last chance to fully round out your draft board before the next class.

## Offseason Week 1

- Contract deadlines hit.
- Expiring contracts roll forward or drop into free agency.
- Dead cap ticks down.
- Fifth-year options can be exercised here.
- Coaches can be fired in the opening window if the rules allow it.

## Offseason Week 2

- The coach market opens.
- Free agency begins in earnest.
- The game starts turning over the first wave of offseason personnel moves.

## Offseason Week 3

- The draft starts.
- Post-draft free agency opens after the class is set.
- AI trading also happens before the draft kicks off.

## Offseason Week 4

- Training camp runs.
- Preseason begins after camp is finalized.

## The Long Game

Across seasons, your franchise builds history:

- ELO tracks team strength over time.
- Power rankings move week to week.
- Rivalry heat accumulates between opponents.
- Narrative arcs track undefeated runs, dynasty pushes, breakout seasons, and revenge games.
- The Almanac keeps the permanent record.
- GM Career tracks your personal legacy.

A dynasty is built across these windows, not in a single draft.
