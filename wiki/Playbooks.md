# Playbooks

Playbooks are the play libraries your team draws from on offense and defense.

## What A Playbook Is

- Offensive playbooks are weighted collections of play IDs.
- Defensive playbooks are weighted collections of defensive packages.
- Each playbook is a reusable template, so you are not building everything from scratch every season.

The built-in library gives you a baseline. Custom playbooks let you tailor that baseline to your roster.

## Offense

The offensive side is built around 13 down-and-distance buckets:

- First down
- Second down
- Third down
- Fourth down
- Plus short, medium, and long situations for each

The game uses the active playbook for each bucket automatically. If you create a custom playbook, the first custom version becomes the default for that side of the ball.

## Defense

Defensive playbooks work the same way, but they are built from package-heavy calls like:

- Base defense
- Run defense
- Pass defense
- Nickel
- Dime
- Blitz
- Goal line
- Prevent

## How To Think About It

- Playbooks decide which plays show up most often.
- Gameplans decide how aggressively those plays are run.
- Playbook fit decides whether the roster actually supports the playbook you are calling.

If your roster is built for power running, a pass-heavy playbook will underperform even if the individual plays look good on paper.

## Good Habits

- Start from a built-in template that already matches your identity.
- Clone before you change weights.
- Keep one or two clear identities, not a kitchen sink of every play type.
- Revisit your playbook when your roster changes at quarterback, along the line, or at receiver.
