# Contracts

Contracts cover extensions, fifth-year options, dead cap, and offseason movement.

## Extensions

You can extend players who have `1-2` years remaining if the window is open.

Extension rules:

- Allowed in preseason, regular season, postseason, and offseason week 1.
- Signing bonus cannot exceed salary.
- Total contract length cannot exceed 5 years.
- The team must still fit under the salary cap.
- The player must be interested enough to accept the offer.

If the player does not want the deal, the extension fails.

## Fifth-Year Options

- Fifth-year options are exercised during offseason week 1.
- They only apply to eligible first-round rookies.
- The option only exercises when the player is good enough and the team can afford it.

## Dead Cap

- Cutting a player with a signing bonus can create dead cap.
- Dead cap obligations tick down across the offseason.
- The cap screen counts dead cap as real cap pressure.

## AI Behavior

AI teams also extend players on their own.

They favor players based on:

- Team direction
- Player overall
- Position need
- Timing window

That means you are not the only team making offseason contract decisions.
